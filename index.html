About Me

I graduated from Austin High School in 2011, and from there I had a really hard time finding a career that I really enjoyed and wanted to pursue with passion.

I left high school to go to Auburn University to pursue a career in Pharmacy. I stayed at Auburn for a year and decided that I really didn't love any of the information I was learning. I still had no idea what I wanted to do, so I left college and started working in various different fields.

My brother had a friend who attended a coding bootcamp, and mentioned that I might like it since I really enjoy a good logic problem. So, I looked around and dabbled into some small things like html and css. I decided to give it a shot and signed up for Nashville Software School. In my time there I fell in love with programming. In 1982, Arthur Szathmary quoted a man he once knew in an edition of Princeton Alumni Weekly that read, "Choose a job you love, and you will never have to work a day in your life." I got lucky and found that career path at Nashville Software School.

The Best Takeaways From Nashville Software School

They put a lot of information in front of us in the time I was there, and I couldn't possibly begin to list everything that I did learn. I will however give you the top three things I got from my time there.

#1 Learning to Learn

After a few weeks into my class, the amount of information in the software industry quickly became awe inspiring. The more I learn the more I realize how much I do not know. Software is also constantly changing. The learning never stops. You have to embrace that, as I quoted above to be perfect is to change often. While at school, one of the main things we were taught was how to teach ourselves new technologies. Maimonides, a medieval Jewish phisolpher, says "Give a man a fish and you feed him for a day; teach a man to fish and you feed him for a lifetime." I have been taught to fish and there is a sea of tech knowledge out there to spend my life exploring.

#2 The Value of Teamwork and Communication

During my time at Nashville Software School, we did many group projects which showed me the advantages of having teammates around. Teamwork helps divide larger problems into smaller task that allows each person to work on specific parts of the problem, increases progress, and the quality of work that can be put out in a certain amount of time. Communication between your team is also extremely important. The late Sydney Harris, an American journalist once said, "The two words information and communication are often used interchangeably, but they signify quite different things. Information is giving out; communication is getting through." A team has to be able to communicate to coordinate efforts efficiently and my understanding of that grew at Nashville Software School.

#3 Basic Front-end and Back-end Foundation

Nashville Software School gave me a really good cornerstone to begin honing my software craft. I gained a basic knowledge of programming concepts that are shared across most languages. Learning a new language is now less about syntax and more about becoming adept at the tools and resources provided in that language. I have already delved into Javascript and C# and can't wait to see where my career path takes me.
